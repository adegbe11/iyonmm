Questions

Everything people ask before deleting themselves from the internet

How do I delete my data from data brokers for free?
Send opt-out requests directly to each broker. It's your legal right under CCPA, GDPR and NDPA, and it costs nothing. The free kit above generates the deletion letters and gives you the data broker opt-out list with direct links. California residents can also use the state's free DROP platform to reach every registered broker with one request.
What is a data broker and how did they get my information?
A data broker collects personal information from public records, social media, apps, loyalty programs and leaked databases, compiles it into a profile, and sells it. You never agreed directly. Your data was bought, scraped or traded upstream. People search sites like Spokeo, Whitepages and BeenVerified are the consumer-facing tip of the industry.
How do I remove my name from Google Search?
Use Google's "Results about you" tool to request removal of results exposing your phone, home address or email. Then remove the source listing at the broker itself, or the result reappears. Tab ③ of the kit walks through both, including Bing.
How long does data broker removal take?
Easy brokers comply within 24 to 72 hours. Under CCPA they legally have 45 days, under GDPR one month. Expect full coverage of the major people search sites in 4 to 6 weeks. Stubborn brokers get follow-ups and regulator complaints.
Why does my information keep coming back online?
Brokers continuously re-ingest public records and purchased datasets, so removed profiles often regenerate within 3 to 6 months. The generated letters demand permanent suppression-list placement, and re-running the kit quarterly keeps you removed.
Is Erasa a free Incogni alternative?
The DIY kit is, yes. Free forever, no signup, no profile limits. The difference in philosophy: your opt-out rights are already free by law, so the paperwork should be too. Optional paid automation adds what no subscription rival offers: proof of every removal and a one-time plan with no recurring billing.
What is California's DROP platform?
DROP, the Delete Request and Opt-Out Platform, launched January 1, 2026 under California's DELETE Act. It lets California residents submit a single free deletion request to every data broker registered in the state. Tab ④ of the kit walks you through it, and covers the out-of-state brokers DROP misses.
Someone posted my address online. What do I do right now?
First, document it with screenshots and URLs. If it's on a people search site, use its opt-out; most remove listings within days, and the kit's letters speed it up. If it's doxxing on social media, report it to the platform, since doxxing violates virtually every platform's rules. If you feel in physical danger, contact local police. Then run the full kit so the source data brokers stop refilling the internet with your address.
